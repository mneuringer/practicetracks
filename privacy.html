Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 23, 2026

Practice Tracks ("the app") is a small, personal-use web app that lets invited members of a choir listen to practice tracks stored in a shared Google Drive folder. This policy explains what data the app touches and what it does (and doesn't) do with it.

What data the app accesses

When you sign in with Google, the app requests a single permission: read-only access to Google Drive (drive.readonly scope). It uses this permission solely to:

  • List the audio files inside the specific shared folder the choir owner has configured.
  • Stream those audio files directly to your browser so you can play them.

The app does not access files outside the configured folder, and it cannot modify, upload, move, or delete anything in your Drive. It also does not access your Google email, contacts, or any other Google service.

Where data goes

Practice Tracks is a client-side static web page. There is no backend server, no database, and no analytics. When you sign in:

  • Google issues an access token directly to your browser.
  • Your browser makes API calls to Google Drive directly — the app's hosting provider never sees them.
  • Audio file content is loaded into your browser's memory for playback only. Nothing is uploaded anywhere or persisted across sessions beyond standard browser caching.

No data is ever collected, stored, or transmitted to the app's author or any third party beyond Google itself. No cookies are set by the app. No tracking pixels. No analytics scripts.

Google user data use, limited use disclosure

Practice Tracks's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically, the app:

  • Uses Google user data only to provide and improve user-facing features of the app (namely, reading and playing your practice tracks).
  • Does not transfer Google user data to third parties except as necessary to provide or improve user-facing features, to comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.
  • Does not use Google user data for serving advertisements.
  • Does not allow humans to read Google user data unless we have your specific consent, the data is necessary for security purposes, the data is aggregated and used for internal operations, or as required by law.

Revoking access

You can revoke Practice Tracks's access to your Google account at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions and removing the app from your list of connected apps. You can also click Sign out inside the app at any time to discard your access token from the current browser session.

Who can use the app

Practice Tracks is not a public service. Only Google accounts that the choir owner has explicitly added to the app's test user allowlist (via the Google Cloud OAuth consent screen) can sign in. Everyone else will be rejected at sign-in.

Children's privacy

Practice Tracks is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new "Last updated" date. Continued use of the app after an update constitutes acceptance of the new policy.
